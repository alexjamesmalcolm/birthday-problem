How can this make sense?

So if there's two people in the room the chance should be 1 in 365, right?

Yes, every birthday comparison has a chance of 1 in 365 of finding the same birthday.

So how many for three people?

We just have to count the number of comparisons we'd have to make. Two people meant one comparison but three people means three comparisons.

We can know this by visualizing the connections.

With three people, or three points, we can count the three lines of comparisons.

Now if you're paying attention these are the classic Triangle Numbers!

The equation for the Triangle Numbers is n(n+1)/2 which is how we get the Exact line.
